Subclasses

Cave Diver

Ranger subclass

Level 3: Subterranean Adept

From your experiences in caves you gain the following features:
Rock climber. You gain a 40ft climbing speed.
Spelunker. You can fit in a space the size of a creature one size smaller without squeezing, and a creature two sizes smaller (but not tiny) while squeezing.
Mineral knowledge. When you make a check to determine the value of an unworked mineral, gem, or rock, you have advantage on the check.

Level 3: Cave Diver Spells

Your connection to the underground has granted the following spells.

Ranger Level Spell
3rd Disguise self
5th Darkvision
9th Meld into stone
13th Stoneskin
17th Wall of stone

You always have these spells prepared.

Level 7: Cave's Stride

You have learned how to move in caves expertly and can move through caves really fast. You gain the following benefits:
Space is unnecessary. Your speed isn't halved when squeezing to fit in a space.
Stone pusher. You have advantage on any check to move a rock, short of an attack roll.
Stone's Speed. Your movement speed increases by 5ft.

Level 11: Cave Disaster Master

You have gained skills to avoid disasters while cave diving; you gain the following benefits:
Fall catch. When you're falling, you can use a reaction to halve the fall damage.
See it first. You gain darkvision out to 120ft and blindsight out to 10ft. If you already have darkvision, its radius increases by 120ft.
Weakness finder. You have advantage on any check to find cracks or weak spots in caves and stone structures.

Level 15: Ultimate Cave Diver

You have spent so much time in the caves you are a master at spelunking. You gain the following benefits:
Expert climber. Your climbing speed increases to 50ft.
Rock solid. You gain resistance to non-magical bludgeoning damage.
Spelunking master. You and your party (if you lead) can no longer get lost in a cave; additionally, you can sustain yourself with enough food and water to survive 7 days in a cave.

Charming Domain

Cleric subclass

Clerics that are a part of the charming domain have high charisma and generally follow the deities Aphrodite, Apollo, Loki, etc. They are very good at charming others and good at resisting it themselves. They also generally have chaotic alignments.

Charming domain spells

Cleric level       spells
1st       Charm person, Command
3rd       Crown of madness, Suggestion
5th       Enemies abound, Hypnotic Pattern
7th       Compulsion, Dominate Monster
9th       Dominate person, Geas

Bonus Cantrip

At 1st level you always know the Friends cantrip and it doesn't count against your known cantrips.

Ability Score Improvement

Also at 1st level your charisma score increases by one.

Channel Divinity: Charming Aura

Starting at 2nd level you can use your channel divinity to make you and your party look better.
As an action, you can present your holy symbol and gain a 10 ft. aura around you for 5 minutes that gives you and anyone of your choice in range +2 to charisma and when they force a creature to make a saving throw to not be charmed a -2 to the roll.

Crazy Charmer

Starting at 6th level any and all saving throws a creature makes to not be charmed by you always has -4 to the roll.

Absurd Charming

At 8th level, your charming becomes even more irresistible, whenever you force a creature to make a saving throw to not be charmed, you can force them to instead of making a wisdom saving throw to make a saving throw of any other mental stat.
You can use this ability 3 times per long rest.
You also gain immunity to being charmed.

Master Charmer

Starting at 17th level, all saving throws to not be charmed by you always have disadvantage.
Your aura also gives immunity to being charmed.

Circle of the Sky

Druid subclass

Level 3: Circle of the Sky Spells

Druid Level Prepared Spells
3rd Feather fall, fog cloud, gust, thunderwave, warding wind
5th Sleet storm, wind wall
7th Ice storm, call lightning
9th Cloudkill, control winds
17th Storm of vengeance

You always have these spells prepared.

Level 3: Lift to the Sky

As a bonus action, you can expend a use of your wild shape to call the winds and gain the following benefits for 10 minutes or until you choose to end it (no action required).
Windswept. Both your movement speed and your jump height increase by 10ft.
Wind control. You can cast gust or fog cloud as a bonus action.
Unmoving. When you are moved against your will (such as shoving) you can use a reaction to not be moved.

Level 6: Avatar of the Sky

While your lift to the sky feature is active you also gain the following benefits.
Touch the sky. You gain a flying speed equal to your speed.
Gaseous. You gain resistance to non-magical bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.

Level 10: Sky's Mastery

Mastery from the sky gains you mastery in some elements. Choose one of the following damage types: acid, cold, lightning, or thunder
You have resistance to it, and whenever you deal damage of that type, treat a 1 on a damage die as a 2.
Whenever you finish a long rest, you can change your choice.

Level 14: From the Sky

You now gain a free use of your lift to the sky feature, which you regain when you finish a long rest, and while it's active you gain the following benefits:
Wind quick. Your fly speed increases to 50ft.
Sky's affinity. You have immunity to the damage type you chose for your Sky's Mastery feature.
Sky's fortitude. Whenever you cast a circle of the sky spell, you or an ally within 30 ft of you gains a number of temporary hit points equal to your level + your wisdom modifier.

College of Glass Breakers

Bard subclass

Some Bards who join the college of glass breakers think they are good at singing but are really bad. Others know they sing terribly, and embrace it. Bards in the college of glass breakers are much better at dancing and playing music than singing. At high levels, even doing simple tasks involving a little singing, can cause nearby creatures to take psychic damage.

Level 3: Psychic Mastery

Whenever you deal psychic damage, you can add your charisma modifier. Additionally, you can also expend a use of your bardic inspiration, to roll the die and add it to the damage.

Level 3: Destructive

You have gotten good at breaking things; whenever you roll any kind of damage against an object or construct, treat a 1 as a 2.

Level 6: Have Heard Worse

From hearing yourself sing, you are affected less by mind hurting sounds. You gain resistance to psychic damage.

Level 14: Glass Breaking Song

Whenever you cast a spell that has a verbal component, or as a bonus action on your turn, you can force all creatures within 30 ft of you to succeed an intelligence saving throw, or take 4d8 psychic damage.
Any glass object within range has a 50 percent chance of shattering. Any creature who cannot hear you automatically succeeds the saving throw. You can use this feature twice per long rest, unless you expend a use of your bardic inspiration to restore one use (no action required).

Artificer subclass

Level 3

Tool Proficiency. Gain proficiency in tinkers tools. If you already proficiency in tinker's tools gain proficiency in an artisan tool set of your choice.
Extended Spell List.

Gadget Smith.
Choose 3 of the listed gadgets to learn how to make.
You can activate a gadget as an action or bonus action if it is within 30 ft of you and can deactivate it as an action if it is within your reach. Gadgets also break down after 1 min of use. Gadget effects only take place while the gadget is activated.
During a long rest, if you have tinkers tools you may make 1 gadget or repair 2. You may only have a number of working gadgets equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1).
Each time you level up choose 1 gadget to learn or level up.

  • Regenerator: Each creature within 5 ft of the regenerator heals 1d4 HP at the start of their turn. +1d4 per gadget level.
  • Force Dome: a 5 ft radius see through dome surrounds the gadget. When you activate this gadget you may form an opening in the dome. If the dome is reduced to 0 HP the gadget is deactivated and destroyed. The dome is immune to all non magical damage, resistant to all damage, and has HP equal to 5x your INT (minimum 1). +5x your INT (minimum 1) HP.
  • Instant Wall: a 5 ft wide, 30 ft long, and 20 ft tall wall appears from the gadget. If a creature is occupying a space that the wall takes up, the creature takes 3d10 force damage and moves to the nearest unoccupied space. If the wall is reduced to 0 HP the gadget is deactivated and destroyed. The wall is immune to all non magical damage, resistant to all damage, and has HP equal to 5x your INT (minimum 1). +5x your INT (minimum 1) HP.
  • Auto-Turret: When you activate this gadget you may designate creatures for the turret to not target. The turret takes its turn after yours. The turret will attack the nearest creature within range on its turn. Gun: INT+PB to hit, (60/120), (1d6+INT) piercing damage. +1d6 piercing damage per level.
  • Manual Turret: When you activate this gadget you or any creature you designate may make attacks with it. Attack rolls made with this use DEX and gain a bonus equal to your INT. Gun: DEX(user's)+INT(yours)+PB(yours) to hit, (60/120), (1d6+DEX(user's)+INT(yours)).
  • Damage Emitter: Each creature within 10 ft of the gadget takes 2d6 of a damage type you choose when you activate it. +1d6 damage per level.
  • Temporal Decelerator: Each creature within up to 5 ft (you choose the radius when you activate the gadget) of the gadget is slowed granting them disadvantage on dexterity saving throws originating from outside the radius, disadvantage on attack rolls targeting creatures outside the radius, creatures outside the radius have advantage on attack rolls targeting creatures inside the radius, and each creature inside the aura has its speed halved. +5 ft max radius per level.
  • Temporal Accelerator: Each creature within up to 5 ft (you choose the radius when you activate the gadget) of the gadget is sped up granting them advantage on dexterity saving throws originating from outside the radius, advantage on attack rolls targeting creatures outside the radius, creatures outside the radius have disadvantage on attack rolls targeting creatures inside the radius, and each creature inside the aura has its speed doubled. +5 ft max radius per level.
  • Earthquaker: The ground in a 10 ft radius around this gadget becomes difficult terrain and each creature in the difficult terrain caused by this gadget must make a DEX ST at the start of its turn against your spell save DC. On a fail the creature is knocked prone.
  • Teleporter: As a bonus action you may teleport to the location of the teleporter if you are within 30 ft of the teleporter. +30 ft range per level.

Level 5

Quick Fix. Once per day you may repair a gadget you are holding as an action.
Infused Gadget. You may use one of your infusions to make a gadget. Gadgets made this way do not break down.

Level 9

More Gadgets. You may now have an additional number of gadgets made equal to your PB.

Level 15

Empowering Gadgets. Gain a bonus to your movement speed equal to twice the number of gadgets you have activated.

Oath of Drug Dealing

Paladin Subclass

Oath of Drug Dealing Spells

Paladin Level Spells
3rd Dissonant Whispers, Hideous Laughter
5th Darkness(Smoke Grenade), Blindness/Deafness
9th Stinking Cloud, Haste
13th Confusion, Phantasmal Killer
17th Cloudkill, Contagion

You always have these spells prepared.

Channel Divinity: Brewing Drugs

At the end of a long rest, you brew some drugs. As a Bonus action, you may expend a use of Channel divinity to administer the drugs to a creature you can touch, granting the creature one of the effects of your choice from the list below.

  • Heal a number of d4s equal to your paladin level
  • The creature gains an additional bonus action on each of its turns for 1 minute
  • The creature takes a number of d4s of Psychic or Poison damage (your choice) on a failed CON saving throw against your spell save DC or half as much on a success.
  • The creature gains advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened for 1 hour
  • The creature gains the paralyzed condition for 1 minute
  • The creature's speed increases or decreases (your choice) by 5 times your CHA modifier for 1 minute

Aura of Drug Tolerance

Starting at 7th level, you constantly emanate drugs from your person, giving those around you drug tolerance while you're not incapacitated. The aura extends 10 feet from you in every direction, but not through total cover. Creatures of your choice within your aura gain resistance to Acid, Poison, or Psychic damage. You may change the damage type on a short or long rest.
At 18th level, the range of this aura increases to 30ft.

Skilled Brewer

Starting at 15th level, crafting consumable items costs half as many resources and requires half as much time for you.

Drug Master

As a Bonus Action, you gain the benefits below for 1 minute or until you end them (no action required). Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a Long Rest. You can also restore your use of it by expending a 5th level spell slot (no action required).

  • Intolerance Aura. When an enemy ends its turn in your aura, it must make a CON Saving Throw against your Spell Save DC. On a failed save, the creature gains vulnerability to poison or psychic damage and loses resistance to that damage type if it has resistance to that damage type until the end of its next turn. However, if it has immunity to the damage type, it remains unaffected.
  • Instant Brew. As a Bonus Action on your turn, you may craft a consumable item that you have the resources to craft.
  • Panacea. When you heal a creature, you may choose to double the amount of HP that is regained by the creature.

Path Of The BIG Monke

Barbarian Subclass

3rd Level: Resourceful

Your time spent in the wilderness has made you very resourceful, you gain proficiency in improvised weapons. Your unarmed strikes gain a d6 damage die. Your unarmed strike damage die increases to a d8 at 6th level, a d10 at 10th level and a d12 at 14th level.

3rd Level: Monkey Mad

You can go into a monkey rage when you rage, doing so causes your body to transform into that of a large monkey. For the duration of your rage, you gain the following benefits;

  • Your size increases to Large
  • You can make an unarmed strike as a bonus action.
  • You can move a creature you are grappling at full speed if it is at least one size smaller than you.
  • You gain a climbing speed equal to your walking speed, if you already have a climbing speed, cry about it.
  • The reach of your melee attacks increases by 5 feet.

However the transformation makes it hard for your monkey brain to understand how to use modern weapons. While you are raging, you do not add your proficiency bonus to attack rolls made with martial weapons.

6th Level: Monkey Fists

Monkey Save DC = 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Constitution Modifier
Once per turn When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike or an improvised weapon you can force them to make a constitution saving throw against your monkey save DC. On a failed save, the target is stunned until the end of your next turn. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus and you regain all expended uses after a short or long rest.
While you are raging, you now deal double damage to objects and structures. Your unarmed strikes count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity.

10th Level: Monkey Make a Noise

When you enter a Monkey Rage, you can let out a loud and intimidating Monkey noise. Every hostile creature within 30 ft of you that can hear you must make a Wisdom saving throw against your Monkey save DC. Creatures that succeed their save suffer no effects. Creatures that fail their save become frightened for 1-minute. Frightened creatures can repeat the saving throw at the end of their turn. This feature can be used once per short or long rest.

Path of the Otherworldly Warrior

Barbarian Subclass

Rage with the power of another plane of existence.
Barbarians that take the Path of the Otherworldly Warrior are imbued with power of the elements or power from outer planes. They generally share at least a similar alignment with the plane. High level barbarians take on aspects of the plane and unleash the wrath of these planes.

Level 3: Aspect of the Planes

At 3rd level you channel your rage from another plane which affects other features. Choose a plane from the plane table to determine these effects.

You know the language associated with that plane, and while you're raging, you have resistance to and your rage damage bonus is that damage type.

Level 3: Planar Spells

You learn a spell based on the plane you chose:
Abyss, Carceri, Limbo, Mechanus, Mount Celestia, Nine Hells, Pandemonium, Feywild, or Shadowfell:
You know the find familiar spell and can cast it only as a ritual. Rather than the normal options, your familiar is determined by the plane you chose and is an imp (Nine Hells), homunculus (Mecanus), quasit (Abyss), Dretch (Carceri), skeleton (Shadowfell), slaad tadpole (Limbo or Pandemonium), sphinx of wonder (Mount Celestia), or sprite (Feywild). But unlike normal, your familiar can attack.
An Elemental Plane:
You always have the Armor of Agathy's spell prepared and instead of its normal damage type, it deals the type of damage associated with the plane you chose. You can cast it even when raging. You can cast it a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

Level 6: Rage of the Planes

At 6th level, whenever you rage you can choose and gain one of the following options:
Attacks of the Planes:
The first time on each of your turns you hit a creature with an attack, the attack does the damage type associated with the plane you chose instead of its normal damage type.
Movement of the Planes:
Difficult terrain doesn't cost you extra movement. Additionally, you can spend half your movement speed to exit any non-magical restraints.
Mastery of the Planes:
Whenever you hit an attack with a weapon you have mastery with, you can use any other mastery property instead.

Level 10: Otherworldly Attacking

At 10th level, while you are raging you are a weapon master; all weapon attacks you make use a die size up (d6 to d8 and d8 to d10 and so forth), if it is a d12 use 2d6 instead of a d20. For a weapon that already uses more than one die, increase just one. Your unarmed strikes now do a d4. Additionally, you can now wield two handed weapons in just one hand.

Level 14: Planar Presence

At 14th level, when you rage (lasting for the duration of the rage), you can gain one of the following effects:
Planar Immunity:
You take on a form as an aspect of the plane. You have immunity to the damage type associated with the plane you chose.
Planar Doom:
Every creature of your choice within 30 ft of you must succeed a dex saving throw (DC 8 plus your Constitution modifier and your proficiency bonus) or take a number of d4s of the damage associated with the plane you chose equal to your rage bonus. You can use a bonus action on your turn to cause this again.
Pain of the Plane:
Any damage you do of the type associated with the plan you chose is doubled.

You can use this feature one time per long rest unless you expend a use of your rage to restore it (no action required).

Level 6: Wild Attacks

Whenever you take the attack action or use your Flurry of Blows, you can replace one of the attacks with a casting of one of your Druid cantrips that has a casting time of an action. You can do this once per turn.

Level 11: Nature's Shield

When you or an ally within 30 ft of you is hit with an attack, you can use a reaction and expend 4 Ki / Focus Points to make a plant shield appear in front of that creature. The plant shield grants +5 to AC until the start of your next turn, potentially turning a hit into a miss. If any creature hits the target while they have the shield with an attack, the attacker takes 2d10 piercing damage.

Level 17: Wrath of Nature

Whenever you hit a melee attack with a Monk weapon or an unarmed strike, you can invoke nature's wrath. You expend 7 Ki / Focus Points and the target of the attack takes an additional 4d10 piercing damage, as vines whip them. If the target is large or smaller, the target then has to make a strength saving throw (DC equals your spell DC). If the target fails, the target is then restrained. If the target succeeds, is huge or is gargantuan, it takes an additional 2d10 piercing damage. A restrained creature takes 1d10 piercing damage at the start of each of their turns, and can make a strength (athletics) check against your spell DC, freeing itself on a success.

Jack of All Trades

Master of none

Characters who are a Jack of all trades have a little skill in everything. They're heavy skill monkeys and have lots of variety in features and techniques for battle. Jack of All Trades comes with a lot of choices, so indecisive people could take forever to make a character with this class.

Note: some features refer to the artificer; the artificer I used was the Unearthed Arcana one from the 2024 rules. The parentheses name is the 2025 Unearthed Arcana name. If you want to use those but not the Unearthed Arcana, consult your DM. It is suggested you then use the equivalent feature from the Tasha's Cauldron of Everything artificer.

Skill proficiencies Choose any 4

Multiclassing in Jack of All Trades:

Gain the following features from the table: Hit point die, proficiency in 2 skills and tools (total), and training with light and medium armor.
For features like Skills in all Classes, you cannot pick features you already have, even if it's from a different class; except for the spell casting feature: treat it as if this class was the class giving the spellcasting feature and use the spellcasting multiclass rules.

Multiclassing out of Jack of All Trades:

You cannot multiclass into a class that you took its version of the spellcasting or weapon mastery feature. If you have any feature given by only one class at level 10 or lower, you cannot take the class that gives that feature.

Class Features:

Level 1: Dabbling in all Classes

Choose 2 different features from other classes granted at 1st level from the list below. You gain that feature. If multiple classes are listed for it, choose which class's version to use. You still can only pick it once. (For the purpose of Jack of All Trades rules affecting spellcasting, consider pact magic a spellcasting feature).

  • Unarmored defense (barbarian or monk)
  • Spellcasting (artificer, ranger, or paladin) / pact magic
  • Second wind
  • Weapon mastery (paladin, ranger, or rogue)
  • Lay on hands
  • Magical tinkering (or tinker's magic)
  • Bardic inspiration

Level 2: Expertise

You gain expertise in 2 skills you already have proficiency in. You gain this feature again at levels 7 and 15.

Level 4: Ability Score Improvement

You gain the ability score improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify. You gain this feature again at Jack of All Trades levels 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, and 16.

Level 5: More Dabbling in all Classes

Choose 1 more feature from the Dabbling in All Classes feature; with the list below included. You gain that feature.

  • Extra attack (barbarian, fighter, monk, paladin, or ranger)
  • Fast movement
  • Faithful steed
  • Uncanny dodge
  • Wild companion
  • Deflect attacks
  • Fighting style (fighter, paladin, or ranger)
  • Jack of All Trades
  • Danger sense
  • Reckless Attack

Level 11: Even More Dabbling in All Classes

Choose 2 more features from the More Dabbling in All Classes feature; with the list below included. You gain that feature.

  • Reliable talent
  • Evasion (monk or rogue)
  • Tactical Master
  • Acrobatic movement
  • Radiant strikes
  • Two extra attacks (if you have the extra attack feature)
  • Flash of genius
  • Feral Instinct

Level 13: Artisan

You have experienced a lot of different types of trades. You gain proficiency in 3 more tools.
In addition, you gain expertise in 2 tools.

Level 17: Master Artisan

You are a master of the trades and gain proficiency in all tools.
In addition, you gain expertise in 2 more tools.

Level 18: Dabbled in All

Choose 1 feature from the Even More Dabbling in All Classes feature; with the list below included. You gain that feature.

  • Indomitable Might
  • Feral Senses
  • Elusive
  • Studied attacks

Level 19: Epic Boon

You gain an Epic Boon feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.
Boon of Skill is recommended.

Level 20: Skill Master

You have hit the epitome of the trades. You gain proficiency in all saving throws. You also now have training in all armor. In addition, you can add your proficiency bonus to initiative rolls.
You also gain Expertise in 3 skills of your choice.
