Rules Changes

Alternative Crits

Moderate change

    When you crit, you can choose to deal max damage + damage die or roll on the Crit table

    1 No effect

    2 Reduce creature's speed by 5 ft

    3 +5 damage

    4 Adv on next attack

    5 Deafen creature for 1 min

    6 +1 attack

    7 Reduce creature's speed by 10 ft

    8 +10 damage

    9 You and 1 ally Adv on next attack

    10 Blind creature

    11 +1 bonus action or attack

    12 Reduce creature's speed to 0

    13 Crit as norma

    14 Adv on next attack for entire party

    15 Stun creature for 1 round

    16 +1 action or bonus action

    17 Crit as normal but multiply by 2

    18 Paralyze creature

    19 Crit as normal but multiply by 5

    20 Roll for 2 effects, if one of the results is a 20 the creature is reduced to 0 HP

Alternative Food

Minor change

Food can have a bonus from +0 to +3, for each bonus your food has gives you +1d4 temp HP. adding spices to food increases its bonus by 1

Alternative Healers' Kits

Minor change

Along with the original effects of Healers' Kits, you can use a charge of a healer's kit to take a short rest in 5 minutes or regain 2 hit dice.

Alternative Swimming

Minor change

Like normal rules, unless you have a swimming speed, your speed swimming is half your speed. Alternatively, if you have on heavy armor and don't have a swimming speed, your speed swimming is instead a third of your speed. Likewise, if you wear heavy armor you don't have training / proficiency with, but have a swimming speed, your speed swimming is still halved.

If you're wearing heavy armor underwater, you have disadvantage on dexterity saving throws, but have advantage on strength saving throws to stay in place.

Weapon underwater rules are the same but are ignored if you are standing on something.

Any amount of cold damage over 20 done to a 5 foot cube of water causes it to freeze for 1 round. The cube has AC 10, and 15 hp.

The resistance to fire damage rule is the same.

Alternative Upcasting

Moderate change

Using higher level spell slots to cast spells can do more than just increase the power of a spell but it can also allow for less precise casting techniques to successfully cast a spell, allowing for faster casting of a spell. As normal, you can use the upcasting rules designated in a spell's description but you can also upcast to increase the speed of casting a spell. When you cast a spell you can cast it 2 levels higher, doing so will turn an action spell into a bonus action spell, a bonus action spell into a reaction (opportunity attack unless the DM states otherwise) spell, or turn a reaction spell into a free action spell. This type of upcasting does not increase the potency of a spell, but you can still use standard upcasting alongside quickening upcasting. You can use quickening upcasting multiple times on the same cast.

For example, using this feature to upcast Fireball to 6th level to cast it as a bonus action and boost its damage to 9d6.

Rules Additions

Naval Combat

Major ruleset

INITIATIVE

To determine which ship goes first in combat, whichever side made the first move starts combat, if you are not sure, roll a d6. On a 1-3, the players go first, on a 4-6 the enemies go first. (If there are more than 2 ships, roll initiative like normal, using the creature with the highest initiative bonus’ bonus as the initiative bonus for the ship.)

ACTIONS IN COMBAT

Each PC can either take a normal turn or a crew action on their turn. The ship gets additional crew actions based on how well manned it is.

  • A fully manned crew (all crew slots filled) gains +3 crew actions.
  • A well-manned crew (75% of crew slots filled) gains +2 crew actions.
  • An undermanned crew (50% crew slots filled) gains +1 crew action.
  • A ship without a crew cannot take crew actions.

DAMAGE CONVERSION

Ships use a different HP and damage system: naval hitpoints (NHP) and naval damage (NDMG). One NHP or NDMG is equivalent to 10 HP or damage.

If you deal NDMG to HP, multiply the rolled damage by 10, and if you deal normal damage to NHP, divide the damage by 10 and round down.

ATTACKING SHIPS

When attacking a ship, choose a specific part of the ship to target that is not the hull. Roll to hit the part of the ship you targeted and the hull. The lower roll is to hit the target you chose, while the higher roll is to hit the hull. If you hit the part you targeted, you don’t deal the damage to the hull.

  • If you have advantage on an attack, roll a third die and drop the lowest of the three.
  • If you have disadvantage, do the same, but drop the highest.
  • If you are using an AOE, you automatically hit the hull and roll to hit the part you targeted as normal.
Ramming:

To ram, the front of your ship (your ram) must be contacting the ship you want to ram. If you are ramming head to head, both ships take (your speed + target’s speed)d4 bludgeoning NDMG. If you are ramming head to side, both ships take (your speed)d4 bludgeoning NDMG. If you are ramming head to back, both ships take (your speed - target’s speed)d4 bludgeoning NDMG.

WEIGHT, SPEED, AND AGILITY

For every 5 weight you have, reduce your speed by 2 and your agility by 1.

Every full cargo slot adds 1 weight, and certain upgrades add weight.

There are 2 types of cargo slots: storage slots and crew slots. A storage slot can hold 1 ton. A crew slot can hold 10 crew members.

ACTIONS

  • Catch the Wind: Move forward up to the ship’s speed. Must be in the rigging.
  • Man the Wheel: Rotate 45 deg, move forward 1, repeat your agility times. Must be at the helm.
  • Sea Shanty: Move half your speed or rotate 45 deg. Must be near the oarsmen.
  • Fire Weapon: Make 1 naval weapon attack. Must be at the location of the weapon. Repeatable.
  • Repair: Heal 1 part of the ship for 1 NHP. Must be at the location of the part you want to repair. Repeatable.
  • Help: Repeat the next player’s action. You cannot help a help action. Repeatable.
  • Move: Move to a different part of the ship. Repeatable.
  • Board: You may move onto a different ship. Must be on the deck. The ship must be adjacent to the ship you are boarding. Repeatable.

Actions that are repeatable can be used more than once each round.

Retaliation Monster Feature

Minor ruleset

Retaliation is a feature that can be given to certain powerful monsters, when you give this feature to a monster, increase its CR by 2. Creatures with the Retaliation feature gain the following benefits:

  • When it is targeted by an attack or put inside an AOE effect it gains a retaliation point.
  • For every 10 HP it loses, it gains a retaliation point.
  • It can have a max number of retaliation points equal to half of its CR (minimum of 1)
  • It can spend 1 retaliation point to take a simple action such as making an attack (not using multiattack) or interacting with an object.
  • It can spend 2 or 3 retaliation points, depending on the action, to use a more complex action such as a dragon's breath weapon.
  • It can spend 4 retaliation points to use a multiattack.
  • Retaliation points can be used at the end of any creature's turn
